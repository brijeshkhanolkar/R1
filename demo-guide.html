🎤 Tough Judge Questions — Prepared Answers
Q: How do you actually connect to ambulances?
A: "Today we use OpenStreetMap data to find nearest hospitals and show ETAs. Production deployment would integrate with private ambulance fleet APIs like Zervice or LifeSign. The good news is private ambulances have GPS and dispatch software — we'd be their demand side, like Uber."
Q: Is the legal protection claim accurate?
A: "Yes — we display the exact text of each country's Good Samaritan law. India: Motor Vehicles Act §134A, 2019. Thailand: Section 374 Thai Penal Code. All 8 BIMSTEC countries are covered with real citations."
Q: What happens without internet?
A: "The app is a PWA with a Service Worker. Core features — legal protection screen, first aid guidance, and emergency SMS — all work offline. The SMS fallback opens the native dialer with GPS pre-filled, so it works on 2G."
Q: How is this different from 108 / 112?
A: "Three things: First, bystanders won't call because of legal fear — we solve that before they even dial. Second, government systems only dispatch government ambulances — we route to private ones that are often 4× faster. Third, we pre-alert the hospital with the victim's medical ID before arrival."
Q: What's your business model?
A: "B2G (Government as customer) — sell to BIMSTEC transport ministries as a white-label emergency platform. Secondary: B2B2C through private ambulance networks who pay per-dispatch fee. Zero cost to bystanders, always."
Q: Is the 52K lives claim real?
A: "The calculation is: 340K annual BIMSTEC road deaths × ~45% occur in the golden hour window × 30% reduction from faster response = ~46K lives. This uses the WHO Golden Hour model. Conservative estimate."
